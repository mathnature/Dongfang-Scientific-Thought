Dongfang Unitary Principle

Abbreviated Version (Physics Academic Context)

There exist definite transformation relationships between different metrics. The natural laws themselves do not change due to the choice of different metrics. When various mathematical representations of the same natural law in different metrics are transformed into one specific metric, the result must be identical to the intrinsic form within that metric, 1=1, and the transformation is unitary.

Extended Version (General Philosophical Context)

Different logical deduction systems for the same proposition possess definite mapping relationships. The objective conclusion of the proposition does not change due to different forms of deduction. When various representations of the proposition's result in different logical systems are transformed into one specific logical system, the result must be identical to the intrinsic representation within that logical system, 1=1, and the transformation is unitary.

Dongfang Logical Entropy Principle

Extended Version (General Philosophical Context)

In a logical system, the total information content carried by all logical elements—including definitions and concepts, preconditions, all necessary and sufficient conditions, as well as all probabilistic and deterministic causal relationships—is termed logical entropy. When the preconditions hold true, the logical entropy of the deduction with the highest confidence is minimized and remains constant.

Abbreviated Version (Mathematical-Physical Logic Context)

In logical deduction systems of mathematical and physical propositions, the total information content constituted by all preconditions, inference rules, and conclusion relationships is termed logical entropy. When the preconditions hold true and the reasoning is valid, the logical entropy of the optimal proof path is minimized and remains constant.

Historical Mission of Dongfang Science

A Civilizational Relay Connecting Past and Future

Europe pioneered the mathematical system and the classical paradigm of natural science, establishing the irreplaceable systemic meta-rules of modern civilization. From the rigorous axiomatic system of Euclid's Elements to the edifice of classical mechanics constructed in Newton's Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy; from the independent creation of calculus by Leibniz and Newton, which ushered in the era of mathematical analysis, to Faraday's revelation of the law of electromagnetic induction through intuitive experimentation, Europe's scientific pioneers laid a solid rational foundation and scientific paradigm for human civilization through systematic theoretical construction and experimental exploration. Particularly Faraday, this bookbinding apprentice of humble origins with almost no formal education, yet with extraordinary intuition and simple experiments, insightfully uncovered nature's deeply hidden electromagnetic mysteries. His discoveries not only directly led the electrical revolution but also became the cornerstone of modern electrical civilization and technological application. His scientific spirit continues to inspire countless explorers today, demonstrating the enduring value of theory and practice.

Diverse and Complementary Scientific Paradigms

The United States' outstanding achievements in applied science and technological innovation demonstrate the immense energy released by the integration of scientific practice and theory. These accomplishments stem not only from substantial R&D investment and an innovative culture but also benefit from an open research ecosystem, rooted in its native soil, that encourages academic freedom and collaboration among elites, providing continuous momentum and paradigmatic reference for global scientific and technological progress.
