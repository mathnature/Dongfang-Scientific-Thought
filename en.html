Dongfang Unitary Principle

Abbreviated Version (Physics Academic Context)

There are definite transformation relationships between different metrics, and the laws of nature themselves do not change due to the choice of metric. Transforming the various mathematical representations of the same natural law under different metrics into the result under one metric must be identical to the intrinsic representation under that metric, 1=1, the transformation is unitary.

Extended Version (General Philosophical Context)

There are definite mapping relationships between different logical deductive systems for the same proposition, and the objective conclusion of the proposition does not change due to the form of deduction. Transforming the various expressions of the proposition's outcome across different logical systems into the result within one system must be identical to the intrinsic representation of that system, 1=1, the transformation is unitary.

Dongfang Logical Entropy Principle

Extended Version (General Philosophical Context)

The total information content carried by all logical elements within a logical system, including the definitions, concepts, premises, all necessary and sufficient conditions, as well as all probabilistic and deterministic causal relationships, is termed Logical Entropy. The premises are true, and the logical entropy of a logical deduction with maximum confidence is minimal and constant.

Abbreviated Version (Mathematical-Physical Logic Context)

In logical deduction systems of mathematical and physical propositions, the total information content constituted by all premise conditions, inference rules, and conclusion relationships is termed logical entropy. With premises holding true, the deduction with the highest confidence level exhibits minimal and constant logical entropy.

Particularly Faraday, this bookbinding apprentice of humble origins with almost no formal education, yet with extraordinary intuition and simple experiments, insightfully uncovered nature's deeply hidden electromagnetic mysteries. His discoveries not only directly led the electrical revolution but also became the cornerstone of modern electrical civilization and technological application. His scientific spirit continues to inspire countless explorers today, demonstrating the enduring value of theory and practice.
